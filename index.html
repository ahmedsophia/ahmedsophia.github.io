My Story

I was always fascinated by an old brown piano that we inherited from my great grandmother in Prague. I found the courage to open it one day and lay my fingers on the black and white keyboard. I practiced for hours until late night when I finally learned to play my first song, the famous Für Elise from Ludwig van Beethoven. It was nowhere near to perfect and some notes might have been incorrect but to my ears it was a wonderful discovery.

My beginning chords and simple songs grew into etudes and some of the most beautiful pieces from famous classical composers thanks to my teacher Mgr. Jitka Šanderová at ZUŠ Lounských in Prague, Czech Republic. Her style, support and commitment to teach me to bring every detail of my play to perfection motivated me to find the strength and courage to step into the world of competitions and concerts.

For every competition my teacher and I had a routine. In preparation, we had to master all the details and beautiful nuances my repertoire could offer. I remember my first competition and all the feelings of excitement, joy, panic, and fear at the same time. I would never forget the moment when the judges called out my name for the first prize. It was very rewarding and motivating. Although I was a winner many times again, it was an honor to be able to play in some of the most beautiful music halls on some of the best grand pianos in the world.

Over the years I learned the techniques to bring out the best sound from an instrument. I realized that each piano's sound is in the hands of the player. I learned to express and convey my emotions through the music, creating an incredible experience for both me, as well as the audience. Playing piano has helped me find my own voice as a musician.

Last year I was approached by a family to teach their young daughter to play the piano. I spent many hours preparing for the first lesson, reviewing the fundamentals of piano technique and selecting even writing my own piano pieces for beginners. It was a challenge to choose a piece that would be suitable for someone I have never met before.

As the first lesson progressed, I observed my student's abilities so I could diligently prepare for our upcoming session. I had to learn how to break down complex concepts into easily understandable steps for a little child. Over the whole year I could watch my student progress and improve, but for me the most rewarding part was to witness her growing love for music.
