My Story

As a little child I was always fascinated by an old brown piano that we inherited from my great grandmother in Prague. I found the courage to open it one day and lay my fingers on the black and white keyboard. I practiced for hours until late night when I finally learned to play my first song, the famous Für Elise from Ludwig van Beethoven. It was nowhere near to perfect and some notes might have been incorrect but to my ears it was a wonderful discovery. Since that day my passion and love for playing the piano has been only growing.

My first chords and simple songs grew quickly into etudes and some of the most beautiful pieces from famous classical composers. I worked very hard on bringing my play to perfection and soon found the strength and courage to step into the world of competitions.

For every competition I had a routine. In preparation, I would always spend many hours planning and mastering all the details and beautiful nuances my repertoire could offer. I became determined to give the best performance and to win. I remember my first competition and all the feelings of excitement, joy, panic, and fear overwhelming me at the same time. I would never forget the moment when the judges called out my name for the first prize. It was very rewarding for me and motivated me to attend other prestigious competitions. Although I was a winner many times again, it was rather an honor to be able to play in some of the most beautiful music halls on some of the best grand pianos in the world.

Over the years I learned the techniques to bring out the best sound from an instrument and learned that each piano is in the hands of the player. It was up to me to bring out the best qualities even if it was not the most fabulous piano. I learned to express and convey my emotions through the music creating an incredible experience for both me, as well as the audience. Playing an instrument has helped me improve in many ways. It enhanced my ability to stay focused and concentrated. I have learned to listen patiently and practice with endurance. But most importantly it is the happiness that it gives me and everyone around.
